Portfolio

JMARS AR Viewer

I lead a team of undergraduate students to develop JMARS AR and VR experiences. We extend JMARS, a planetary GIS that provides mission planning and data analysis tools, to visualize planetary terrains in AR and VR. The JMARS AR Viewer app is free on the App and Google Play stores.

ASU Commencement App

My colleages and I were asked to help make virtual ASU Commencement a little more personal this year, by making an augmented reality app that brought the commencement stage to students' living rooms. We captured the speakers' point clouds with Microsoft Kinects and callibrated them for use in AR, to give off the illusion that the speaker is right there in your home. We plan to update and add features to the app for future commencements.

Live Point Cloud Shading

I experimented with 3D capture throughout the semester for one of my academic courses. In Max MSP, I made a patch that lets me interact with variants of a point cloud shader and edit my point cloud in real time. This video is a mashup of some of my favorite effects I got while doing a live 3D capture with my sister, who majors in dance. We plan to collaborate and update this work in progress.

Planetary Visor - Research

Planetary Visor is a scientific tool used to visualize orbital and ground data around NASA's Curiosity rover's traverse on Mars. I lead the software development team on and research immersive scientific visualization and interaction methods.

Musical Raspberry Pi

I created a musical composition out of sounds curated in Java with the help of the Happy Brackets toolkit. I made use of the accelerometer on the Raspberry Pi hat to control which pattern to play.

Hexx

Hexx is an interactive installation that unites the audience with live electronic music. We set up motion tracking inside of the structure and use that data in Ableton Live, using a Max for live plugin that we created. The motion is then an instrument or audio effect of choice for the musician. It can be plugged into Ableton Live for multiple uses.

Cake AR

This was just a fun little iOS game that I made in mobile development class when I was dabbling with multiplayer experiences and augmented reality for the first time. It was recognized at the Digital Culture Showcase in Spring 2019.

Timeline Installation

I made an interactive timeline to show my home videos from when I was a one year old to present (2019). When I got older, the videos transitioned to experiences I recorded, rather than someone recording me, and you can start to see life through my lenses. The pillows have sensors in them that allow me to traverse through the videos when I tap them. I chose this type of interaction to make the experience feel softer and more personal.

Video Editing

In film editing class, we had to make a documentary of ourselves. I thought my awkward dances would speak louder than words. I don't know if I had more fun filming it, dancing in it, or editing it. It was recognized as one of the top films in class.

About Me
